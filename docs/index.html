Triangle Sports Analytics Competition

Compete in predicting ACC basketball point spread

There are two different competitions below. See each for more information! Enter one competition by January 30th date.

Represent your school in data science and to take home the title!

The Triangle Sports Analytics Competition aims to bring together students who love sports analytics to compete in a prediction contest. In this competition, participants are able to showcase their skills in one of two different prediction challenges: point spreads and prediction intervals. Students may work individually or in a team of 2-5.

The most exciting aspect of this competition is that ALL ACC Men’s Basketball games between January 31, 2026 and March 7, 2026 will be the focus of this competition. If you plan on following your university basketball team’s fight to the NCAA tournament, why not fight for your university’s reputation in data science by competing in the 2nd annual Triangle Sports Analytics Competition.

The Competitions

The winners of each competition will receive:

Predicting point spread
(entry level)

Point spread is the difference in points scored between Team1 and Team2. Your job is to create a statistical model that predicts the point spread of all ACC basketball games (# total). To enter, you need to:

  • Find data
  • Build a model
  • Make predictions
  • Submit a spreadsheet
  • Write a paragraph on your process & submit it!

You have the opportunity to change your model, and re-submit predictions in February!

Details about the contest and how to get started can be found here - Challenge: Point Spread.

Predicting point spread + intervals
(advanced level)

Point spread is the difference in points scored between Team1 and Team2. Your job is to create a statistical model that predicts the point spread AND create prediction intervals of all ACC basketball games (# total). To enter, you need to:

  • Find data
  • Build a model
  • Make predictions
  • Make prediction intervals
  • Submit a spreadsheet
  • Write a paragraph on your process & submit it!

You have the opportunity to change your model, and re-submit predictions in February!

Details about the contest and how to get started can be found here - Challenge: Point Spread + Interval.

Entry Requirements

All Duke, NC State, and UNC students are encouraged to participate in one challenge. Students may work individually or in a team of 2-5.

All submissions for both challenges will be due at 11:59PM on January 30th. Groups unable to submit on time will be ineligible for participation. This is to promote a fair competition.

Furthermore, we ask you follow the submission guidelines found on both the Challenge: Spread page and the Challenge: Interval page. If your submission is on time but requires a considerable amount of data cleaning, we may allow you to resubmit after you fix the problems, or you may be disqualified from the challenge.

Contact for Questions

If you have any questions, first see our FAQ page.

If your question(s) remain unanswered, contact either Alexander Fisher (Duke), Elijah Meyer (NC State), or Mario Giacomazzo (UNC). All contact information is found on the FAQ page.
